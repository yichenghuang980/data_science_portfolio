Business Scenario

Purdue Mobile Food Consulting (PMFC) is a big data consulting firm headquartered in West Lafayette, Indiana. The company provides advanced insight to food truck vendors that enables them to make well-informed and analytically-driven business decisions to enhance their position in their respective locations or sectors. Sophisticated data capture on food truck locations, suppliers, events, and food trucks themselves allows PMFC to provide clients with an elevated and meaningful view of the entire industry.

PMFC has information on a number of potential food truck locations. Each location can be occupied by no food trucks, a single food truck, or many food trucks. A food truck must operate in at least one location, but since it is mobile, it can operate in multiple locations over time.

Events are common places in which food trucks are found. At an event, there can be no food trucks or there can be many of them. A particular food truck can choose to operate at no events, or can travel to multiple events. Food trucks rely on suppliers to provide them with inventory. Due to contractual agreements, each food truck can have one and only one supplier. A particular supplier can supply no food trucks, a single food truck, or many food trucks.

Tables and Entities

Four tables in total including locations, trucks, suppliers, events

For locations: locationID (primary key), zone, areaSize, and authorization

For food trucks: truckID (primary key), rating, truckname, trucktype, and sales

For suppliers: supplierID (primary key), suppliername, suppliertype, and location

For events: eventID (primary key), eventname, eventtype, date, fax, and location

Example Solution

Q: PMFS wants to know which truck type performs best in attracting customers and promoting sales during each event so that it can plan more specifically and deploy that truck type more in the incoming events.

List the truck type that has the highest average sales for each event type.

SELECT EventType, TruckType, NoofTrucks, 
        Max(AVGTruckSales) AS TopAVGSalesPerTruck
FROM 
(SELECT event.EventType, foodtruck.TruckType, 
        COUNT(foodtruck.TruckID) AS NoofTrucks,    	
        AVG(foodtruck_event.EventSales) AS AVGTruckSales
FROM event
INNER JOIN foodtruck_event 
ON event.EventID = foodtruck_event.EventID
INNER JOIN foodtruck
ON foodtruck.TruckID = foodtruck_event.TruckID
GROUP BY event.EventType, foodtruck.TruckType
ORDER BY event.EventType DESC, AVG(foodtruck_event.EventSales)
DESC
) A
GROUP BY EventType
ORDER BY TopAVGSalesPerTruck DESC;
